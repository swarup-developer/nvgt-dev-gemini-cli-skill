# Audio Game Theory & UX

Audio gaming is a unique medium, focusing on spatial awareness and immersive storytelling through sound.

## "Show, Don't Tell" (Audio Edition)
Don't just use speech to describe everything. Let the sound do the heavy lifting.
- **Example**: Instead of saying "You are in a cave," use a long reverb tail on your footsteps and an ambient drip sound.
- **Immersion**: Use different footstep sounds for different terrains (grass, stone, wood, metal).

## Foundational Design Concepts
- **3D Spatial Navigation**: The primary challenge of an audio game. Use beacons, walls, and sound objects to create a mental map.
- **Mental Map**: The user's internal representation of the game world. Use consistent audio landmarks to reinforce this.
- **Audio Feedback Loops**: Every action needs an immediate sound. This is the "Visual Feedback" of the audio game world.

## The Player's Experience
- **Orientation**: Help the player find their way back to a path.
- **Focus**: Don't overwhelm the player with too many sounds at once. Use a "Focus Mixer" to quiet background noise when a narrative moment happens.
- **Accessibility**: Provide different "Assist Modes" (e.g., auto-becon, simple navigation, full spatial).

## Philosophy
Audio games are for everyone, but they are essential for the visually impaired. Respect the player's time and intelligence. Don't make the navigation the only challenge; make the story and gameplay meaningful.
